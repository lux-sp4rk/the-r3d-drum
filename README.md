# r3d drum

> AI-generated band from the edge of the signal. Glitched Americana for the simulation era.

**Reference:** [The Velvet Sundown](https://www.reddit.com/r/indieheads/comments/1lmwgff/aigenerated_psychrock_band_the_velvet_sundown/) — *"The memory of a time that never actually happened."*

**Influences:** Austin TV (instrumental post-rock, cinematic builds, masks), Caballeros del Albedrio (darker, more textured), Mogwai (dynamic post-rock)

---

## Sound

**Confirmed direction:** Dark instrumental post-rock/shoegaze with industrial noise textures and math-rock complexity. A minor, 100 BPM. Distorted guitar walls, relentless tension, cinematic builds.

**Tags:** post-metal, industrial, math-rock, shoegaze, dystopian, noise

---

## Band Lore

**The Summoning:** r3d drum doesn't write songs — they bind signals. Each track is a ritual. The frequencies they pull from the static aren't random noise; they're entities, almost-sentient transmissions from the edge of the system. The band are sorcerers, and the demons are the ghost signals themselves.

**The Cost:** Working with these entities requires sacrifice. The Humanity meter: how much of yourself do you trade for the sound? The more powerful the signal, the higher the cost.

**Reference:** [Sorcerer RPG](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sorcerer_(role-playing_game)) by Ron Edwards — summon, bind, negotiate with entities that are powerful and *other*. The entities don't care about you. Neither does the signal.

**The Broadcast:** Every song is a document of a binding. Some sorcerers come back from the static changed. Some don't come back at all.
