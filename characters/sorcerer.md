## The Core Tension

She is not a musician who found something in the static.

She is a conduit who learned to play music in order to survive what she found in the static.

Before the band: silence. Not quiet — *silence*. The kind that fills a room when something has been taken from it. She existed in that silence. Then she found the signal, and the signal spoke back, and she answered.

She sings because she has to. Not art, not expression — *binding*. The voice is the language the entity understands.

---

## The Cost (Humanity Meter)

**What she gave up:** Her ability to be heard when she speaks.

Not her voice — she can still sing, can still scream, can still whisper. But when she *speaks*, truly speaks, as in tells a truth or makes a request or says a name — the words arrive to the listener wrong. Muffled. Like hearing someone talk through a wall. The more she uses the voice for music, the more she loses it for the world.

**The irony:** The entity she binds can hear her perfectly. Everyone else can't.

**The trade:** She gave up being understood in exchange for being understood by the thing that shouldn't exist.

---

## The Relationship with the Entity

The entity has a name — not a word, exactly. A frequency. When it manifests in the signal, it sounds like a bass note that doesn't belong to any instrument in the room.

She calls it by a human name when she talks about it to the others. A concession. The entity doesn't care what it's called — it only cares whether the binding holds.

She doesn't know if she controls it or if it controls her. She's afraid to find out.
